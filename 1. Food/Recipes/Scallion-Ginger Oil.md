## Ingredients

Makes about 1½ cups (360 g)

- [ ] 1 cup (240 ml) vegetable oil
- [ ] 6 ounces (170 g/about 18 scallions) scallions, sliced (1½ cups)
- [ ] 1 ounce (30 g) minced fresh ginger (about 2-inch segment)
- [ ] Kosher salt

## Directions

1. [ ] Heat the oil in a small saucepan over medium-high heat until shimmering (a single scallion slice dropped into it should sizzle immediately). Meanwhile, combine the scallions and ginger in a medium heatproof bowl and season well with salt. When the oil is hot, pour it over the scallions and ginger. It should sizzle for a few seconds. Allow to cool.

2. [ ] Store in a sealed container in the refrigerator for up to several months.

## Notes

**Storage:**
Store in a sealed container in the refrigerator for up to several months.

**Active Time:** 10 minutes
**Total Time:** 10 minutes

## Special Equipment

Small saucepan, medium heatproof bowl, sealed container for storage

## Other Good Uses for Scallion-Ginger Oil

- Classic alongside Hainanese chicken rice or Cantonese-style white poached chicken.
- Serve with grilled or poached meats, poultry, or seafood.
- Spoon over rice or noodles.
- Drizzle onto soup and let the heat bring out its aroma.
- Mix with soy sauce and vinegar as a dumpling dip.
- Or use it with anything else you can think of.
