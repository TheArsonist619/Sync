## Ingredients
servings: 3 servings (as a side dish)

**Sweet Miso Glaze:**
- [ ] 3 Tbsp miso (white miso recommended)
- [ ] 1 Tbsp sugar
- [ ] 1 Tbsp mirin
- [ ] ½ Tbsp sake (or water)

**Eggplant:**
- [ ] 3 Japanese eggplants (1.2 lb / 518 g)
- [ ] 1 Tbsp toasted sesame oil

**Garnish (Optional):**
- [ ] 1 green onion/scallion
- [ ] 5 shiso leaves (perilla/ooba)
- [ ] 2 tsp toasted white sesame seeds

## Directions

1. [ ] Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Position a rack at the center, approximately 7 inches from the heating element.
2. [ ] Combine the miso and sugar in a small bowl. Mix in the mirin and sake until you reach the desired consistency.
![[misoeggplant1.webp|400]]
3. [ ] Cut the eggplants in half lengthwise. Score the cut surfaces diagonally in a crisscross pattern (⅛ inch deep).
![[misoeggplant2.webp|400]]
4. [ ] Soak the eggplants in water for 10 minutes. Blot dry with paper towels.
5. [ ] Brush the cut surfaces with sesame oil. Place cut-side down on a parchment-lined baking sheet.
6. [ ] Bake 15 minutes, until the flesh softens and the skin slightly shrivels.
7. [ ] Preheat the broiler.
8. [ ] Turn the eggplants cut-side up. Brush the flesh with the miso glaze.
![[misoeggplant3.webp|400]]
9. [ ] Broil 3–5 minutes, until the glaze bubbles and caramelizes.
10. [ ] Sprinkle with sesame seeds, sliced green onions, and julienned shiso. Serve immediately.

## Notes

- **Storage:** Refrigerate leftovers in an airtight container up to 3 days. Store leftover glaze in the fridge up to 7 days, or freeze up to 1 month.
- **Sake substitute:** Water can stand in for the sake in the glaze.
