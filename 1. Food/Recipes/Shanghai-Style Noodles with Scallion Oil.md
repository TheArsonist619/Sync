## Ingredients

Serves 2

- [ ] 1 pound (450 g) fresh or dried noodles
- [ ] ¼ cup (60 ml) [[Shanghai-Style Scallion Oil]] (along with some of the scallion pieces)
- [ ] 2 tablespoons (30 ml) light soy sauce or shoyu
- [ ] 1 tablespoon (15 ml) dark soy sauce
- [ ] 2 teaspoons (8 g) sugar

## Directions

1. [ ] Cook noodles according to package directions.

2. [ ] Rinse the noodles briefly under cold water when they are done to stop cooking.

3. [ ] In a wok or large saucepan, heat the scallion oil (along with some of the scallion pieces), light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and sugar over high heat until the sugar dissolves and the sauce is simmering.

4. [ ] Add the noodles, toss to coat in the sauce, and cook until just heated through.

5. [ ] Transfer to a serving platter and serve. You can top with more of the fried scallions from the jar of scallion oil if you'd like.

## Notes

**Noodle Options:**
Works well with fresh or dried noodles. Fresh noodles cook faster.

**Sauce Ratio:**
The recipe is easily scalable — for every 2 portions of noodles, use the oil and sauce amounts listed.

**Garnish:**
Top with additional fried scallions from the jar for extra flavor and texture.

**Active Time:** 10 minutes
**Total Time:** 15 minutes

## Special Equipment

Wok or large saucepan
