## Ingredients
servings: 4 (makes about 20 fritters)

- [ ] 1 pound taro root, peeled
- [ ] 4 scallions, trimmed and very thinly sliced
- [ ] 3 large garlic cloves, finely chopped
- [ ] 1 Scotch bonnet or habanero chile, finely chopped
- [ ] 1 large egg, lightly beaten
- [ ] Large handful parsley, finely chopped
- [ ] 1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves
- [ ] 2 teaspoons kosher salt
- [ ] 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
- [ ] Extra-virgin olive oil, for shallow frying
- [ ] 1 juicy lime, halved, or pikliz (page 285), for serving

## Directions

1. [ ] Using a metal box grater, grate the taro on the side with the smallest holes. Scrape the pulp into a medium bowl, add the scallions, garlic, chile, egg, parsley, thyme, salt, and pepper, and stir really well. The batter keeps in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days.
2. [ ] Fill a large, heavy skillet with 1/4 inch of olive oil and heat over medium heat until shimmery. Line a plate with paper towels and put it near the stove.
3. [ ] Next, form the fritters one at a time, adding them to the oil as they're formed. Using a dinner knife, scoop out a blade full of batter, gently patting the batter so it stays put, to create a long, skinny fritter that's about 4 inches long and 3/4 inch high. Transfer it to the oil by holding the knife right above the oil and slowly letting the batter slide off the knife, giving it a little nudge if it sticks. Repeat with the remaining batter, but work in several batches to avoid crowding the pan and leave about 1/2 inch between each fritter.
4. [ ] Fry over medium heat, carefully flipping the fritters so each side gets a turn in the hot oil, until crispy and golden brown all over and tender and creamy in the center, about 3 minutes per side. Transfer them to the prepared plate as they're done.
5. [ ] Eat right away with a squeeze of lime or some pikliz.

## Notes

You occasionally see people recommend using gloves to handle taro to avoid the slightly sticky substance released when you peel it (similar to butternut squash). If you're afraid it might irritate your hands, go ahead and use gloves or look for it pre-peeled in Asian markets.
