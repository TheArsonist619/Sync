## Ingredients

Makes 12 ounces

- [ ] 8 ounces (225 g) ground pork
- [ ] 4 ounces (120 g) raw shrimp, roughly chopped into ¼-inch pieces
- [ ] 1 tablespoon (15 ml) light soy sauce
- [ ] 2 teaspoons (5 g) minced garlic (about 2 medium cloves)
- [ ] 2 teaspoons (5 g) minced fresh ginger (about ½-inch segment)
- [ ] 1 teaspoon (4 g) sugar
- [ ] 1 teaspoon (3 g) cornstarch
- [ ] ¼ teaspoon baking soda

## Directions

1. [ ] Combine all the ingredients in a bowl and mix vigorously with your fingertips until the mixture feels slightly tacky, about 1 minute.

2. [ ] Store in a sealed container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days before using or transfer to zipper-lock bags, press flat, squeeze out the air, seal the bags, and freeze for up to 3 months.

## Notes

**Uses:**
This versatile seasoned mixture can be used as:
- The filling for dumplings or wontons (page 414)
- A topping for crispy fried scallion pancakes (page 396)
- A soup for crispy panfried noodles (page 359)
- Forming into balls and dropping them into simmering broth
- In place of shrimp filling for stuffed peppers with black bean sauce (page 150)

**Flavor Profile:**
The flavorings are savory and a little sweet. White pepper adds heat; soy sauce, garlic, and ginger add aroma; sugar adds sweetness; cornstarch helps it retain moisture as it cooks; and a dash of baking soda gives the pork a bouncier texture and the shrimp a firm, juicy crunch.

**Storage:**
Pressed flat and stored in the freezer, the mix will defrost overnight in the fridge, in an hour or so on a rimmed baking sheet on the counter, or in about 10 minutes in a bowl of water in the sink.

**Active Time:** 5 minutes
**Total Time:** 5 minutes
