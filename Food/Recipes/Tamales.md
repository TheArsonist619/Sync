## Ingredients


- [ ] 4 lbs Tri Tip
- [ ] 4 cloves
- [ ] 1/2 onion
- [ ] 3 oz(CHECK) California chiles
- [ ] 3 oz(CHECK) Guajillo chiles
- [ ] 2 handfuls(Candi sized) chile de arbol
- [ ] Hojas
**Sauce**
- [ ] 1/2 onion
- [ ] 5 garlic cloves
- [ ] 1/2 tsp cumin
- [ ] tbsp mexican oregano
## Directions

1. [ ] Put hojas in large pot of hot water and allow to sit all day.
2. [ ] Boil trip tip with cloves and onion with enough water to cover. Add salt after about an hour or so. Simmer for 2-4 hours, until fork tender. Pull out meat and allow to cool but **SAVE broth for sauce**. Shred. 
3. [ ] Peel chile stems and seeds, rinse and bring to a boil.  Once boil starts, turn off heat and allow to soften.
4. [ ] Shallow fry chiles de arbol until toasted for 2-3 minutes. Constantly stir to keep them from burning
5. [ ] Blend all chiles(add chile de arbol to taste) with 2-3 cups of reserved broth. Adding more liquid and chile de arbol as needed.
6. [ ] Heat dutchoven back up with 1/4 of oil. Fry meat in oil for a bit then add all salsa(and more broth if needed)with 3-5 bay leaves . Season to taste with salt, pepper and knorr chicken. Cook for a bit. Allow to chill
7. [ ] Make Masa(!!!)
8. [ ] Rinse corn husks and allow to drain while making tamales
9. [ ] Spoon 1/4 or so into husk and spread *thin* on husk, using a wet spoon to spread it so it doesn't stick. Fold over
10. [ ] When steaming, place extra husks on top of steamer to help keep steam in.
