## Ingredients

Makes 1 cup

- [ ] 1 cup (150 g) raw peanuts
- [ ] 1 cup peanut, rice bran, or other neutral oil

## Directions

1. [ ] Combine the peanuts and oil in a wok. Heat over medium-high heat, stirring occasionally, until the peanuts start to bubble gently. Reduce the heat to maintain a very gentle bubble around the peanuts (the oil should register around 250°F on an instant-read thermometer) and cook, stirring occasionally, until the peanuts are a light, even golden brown and have a toasty aroma, about 20 minutes.

2. [ ] Retrieve the peanuts with a fine-mesh strainer or metal spider and transfer to a rimmed baking sheet lined with a few layers of paper towels or a clean kitchen towel. Let the peanuts cool a little, then blot off excess oil with more paper towels or a kitchen towel. When completely cool, transfer the peanuts to a sealed container and store in a cool, dark pantry for 2 to 3 months. (The peanuts will start to smell a little fishy if they have turned rancid. They are still safe to eat; they just won't taste very good.)

## Notes

**Usage:**
Peanuts make frequent appearances alongside noodles to add crunch and nutty flavor. The easiest way to incorporate them into your cooking is to buy jars of roasted salted peanuts, which can be lightly crushed in a mortar and pestle or under a heavy skillet before sprinkling on your noodles.

For better flavor and texture, try frying your own raw peanuts. They get considerably crispier and have a deeper, nuttier aroma than regular roasted peanuts. They're also easy to do in bulk and last a long time in a cool, dark pantry.

**Temperature Control:**
Using an instant-read thermometer is recommended to maintain the oil at around 250°F. This low temperature prevents burning while allowing the peanuts to cook through evenly.

**Storage:**
Store in a cool, dark pantry for 2 to 3 months. If peanuts turn rancid, they'll smell fishy but are still technically safe to eat, just not flavorful.

**Active Time:** 5 minutes
**Total Time:** 25 minutes

## Special Equipment

Wok, fine-mesh strainer or metal spider, rimmed baking sheet, paper towels, instant-read thermometer, sealed container for storage

## Hot and Numbing Fried Peanuts

A quick variation using the fried peanuts with the Hot and Numbing Spice Blend.

### Ingredients

- [ ] 1 cup Fried Peanuts (see above)
- [ ] 2 to 4 tablespoons Hot and Numbing Spice Blend (page 185)

### Directions

1. [ ] Toss the freshly fried peanuts with 2 to 4 tablespoons of the Hot and Numbing Spice Blend, depending on desired spice level.

2. [ ] Let the mixture cool completely and store in sealed containers in the pantry for several weeks.

### Notes

This variation makes an excellent snack or bar food and can be used as a topping for noodles or other dishes where you want extra heat and numbing sensation.
